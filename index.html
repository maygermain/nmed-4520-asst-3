Distance

Galaxies from the Messier Catalogue and their distance from Earth

What is a kilo light year?

A light year is the distance light travels in one year - about 9,460,000,000,000 (9.46 trillion) kilometers!

For context, if you were to fly around the earth, it would only be about 40,075km.

A light year is roughly equivalent to flying around the earth 236 million times.

A kilo light year is a thousand light years - 9,460,000,000,000,000 (9.46 quadrillion) kilometers.

The distance between us and the galaxies of the Messier catalogue are incredibly vast, but all of them are visible from earth using relatively simple equipment.

That's all!

The Messier catalgue contains a number of galaxies somewhat easily viewed from earth, but these galaxies are just the beginning. An image shot by the James Webb Telescope captured thousands of galaxies in a patch of sky no larger than a grain of sand.

As of 2006, the observable universe was believed to be 94 billion light years, or 94 million kilo light years - far, far larger than the scope of the galaxies in the Messier catalog.
